irreducible polynomials over finite field


Theorem.  Over a finite field F, there exist irreducible polynomials of any degree.

Proof.  Let n be a positive integer, p be the characteristic of F, 𝔽p be the prime subfield, and pr be the order (http://planetmath.org/FiniteField) of the field F.  Since pr-1 is a divisor of pr⁢n-1, the zeros of the polynomial Xpr-X form in  G:=𝔽pr⁢n  a subfield isomorphic to F.  Thus, one can regard F as a subfield of G.  Because

[G:F]=[G:𝔽p][F:𝔽p]=r⁢nr=n,

the minimal polynomial of a primitive element of the field extension G/F is an irreducible polynomial of degree n in the ring F⁢[X].
