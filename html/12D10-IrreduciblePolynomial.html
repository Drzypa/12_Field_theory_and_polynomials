irreducible polynomial


Let  f⁢(x)=a0+a1⁢x+⋯+an⁢xn  be a polynomial with complex coefficients aν and with the degree (http://planetmath.org/Polynomial)  n>0.  If f⁢(x) can not be written as product of two polynomials with positive degrees and with coefficients in the field  ℚ⁢(a0,a1,…,an),  then the polynomial f⁢(x) is said to be .  Otherwise, f⁢(x) is reducible.

Examples.  All linear polynomials are .  The polynomials x2-3, x2+1 and x2-i are (although they split in linear factors in the fields ℚ⁢(3), ℚ⁢(i) and ℚ⁢(1+i2), respectively).  The polynomials x4+4 and x6+1 are not .

The above definition of polynomial is special case of the more general setting where f⁢(x) is a non-constant polynomial in the polynomial ring K⁢[x] of a field K; if f⁢(x) is not expressible as product of two polynomials with positive degrees in the ring K⁢[x], then f⁢(x) is (in K⁢[x]).

Example.  If K is the Galois field with two elements (0 and 1), then the trinomial x2+x+1 of K⁢[x] is (because an equation  x2+x+1=(x+a)⁢(x+b)  would imply the two conflicting conditions  a+b=1  and  a⁢b=1).
