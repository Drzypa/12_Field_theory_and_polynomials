quadratic formula


The roots of the quadratic equation

a⁢x2+b⁢x+c=0  a,b,c∈ℝ,a≠0

are given by the formula

x=-b±b2-4⁢a⁢c2⁢a.

The number Δ=b2-4⁢a⁢c is called the discriminant of the equation. If Δ>0, there are two different real roots, if Δ=0 there is a single real root, and if Δ<0 there are no real roots (but two different complex roots).

Let’s work a few examples.

First, consider 2⁢x2-14⁢x+24=0. Here a=2, b=-14, and c=24. Substituting in the formula gives us

x=14±(-14)2-4⋅2⋅242⋅2=14±44=14±24=7±12.

So we have two solutions (depending on whether we take the sign + or -): x=82=4 and x=62=3.

Now we will solve x2-x-1=0. Here a=1, b=-1, and c=-1, so

x=1±(-1)2-4⁢(1)⁢(-1)2=1±52,

and the solutions are x=1+52 and x=1-52.
