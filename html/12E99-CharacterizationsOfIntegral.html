characterizations of integral


Theorem.

Let R be a subring of a field K,  1∈R  and let α be a non-zero element of K.  The following conditions are equivalent:

  1. 1.

    α is integral over R.

  2. 2.

    α belongs to R⁢[α-1].

  3. 3.

    α is unit of R⁢[α-1].

  4. 4.

    α-1⁢R⁢[α-1]=R⁢[α-1].

Proof.  Supposing the first condition that an equation

αn+a1⁢αn-1+…+an-1⁢α+an=0,

with aj’s belonging to R, holds.  Dividing both by αn-1 gives

α=-a1-a2⁢α-1-…-an⁢α-n+1.

One sees that α belongs to the ring R⁢[α-1] even being a unit of this (of course  α-1∈R⁢[α-1]).  Therefore also the principal ideal α-1⁢R⁢[α-1] of the ring R⁢[α-1] coincides with this ring.  Conversely, the last circumstance implies that α is integral over R.
