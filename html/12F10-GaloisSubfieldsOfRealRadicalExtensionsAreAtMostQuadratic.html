Galois subfields of real radical extensions are at most quadratic


Theorem 1.

Suppose F⊂L⊂K=F⁢(αn)⊂R are fields with α∈F and L Galois over F. Then [L:F]≤2.

Proof. Let ζn be a primitive nth root of unity, and define F′=F⁢(ζn), L′=L⁢(ζn), and K′=K⁢(ζn)=F′⁢(αn).

\xymatrix⁢@⁢R⁢1⁢p⁢c⁢@⁢C⁢.3⁢p⁢c⁢&⁢K′=K⁢(ζn)=F′⁢(αn)⁢\ar⁢@-[d⁢l]⁢\ar⁢@-[d⁢r]⁢&⁢&⁢K=F⁢(αn)⁢\ar⁢@-[d⁢r]⁢&⁢&⁢L′=L⁢(ζn)⁢\ar⁢@-[d⁢l]⁢\ar⁢@-[d⁢r]⁢&⁢&⁢L⁢\ar⁢@-[d⁢r]⁢&⁢&⁢F′=F⁢(ζn)⁢\ar⁢@-[d⁢l]⁢&⁢&⁢F⁢&

Now, L′/F′ is Galois since L/F is. But K′ is a Kummer extension of F′, so has cyclic Galois group and thus L′/F′ has cyclic Galois group as well (being a quotient of Gal⁡(K′/F′)). Thus L′ is a Kummer extension of F′, so that L′=F′⁢(βn) for some β∈F′. It follows that L=F⁢(βn). But since L is Galois over F, it follows that n≤2 (since otherwise in order to be Galois, L would have to contain the non-real nth roots of unity).
