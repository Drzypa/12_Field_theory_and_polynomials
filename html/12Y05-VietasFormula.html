Vieta’s formula


Suppose P⁢(x) is a polynomial of degree n with roots r1,r2,…,rn (not necessarily distinct). For 1≤k≤n, define Sk by

Sk=∑1≤α1<α2<…⁢αk≤nrα1⁢rα2⁢…⁢rαk

For example,

S1=r1+r2+r3+…+rn
S2=r1⁢r2+r1⁢r3+r1⁢r4+r2⁢r3+…+rn-1⁢rn

Then writing P⁢(x) as

P⁢(x)=an⁢xn+an-1⁢xn-1+…⁢a1⁢x+a0,

we find that

Si=(-1)i⁢an-ian

For example, if P⁢(x) is a polynomial of degree 1, then P⁢(x)=a1⁢x+a0 and clearly r1=-a0a1.

If P⁢(x) is a polynomial of degree 2, then P⁢(x)=a2⁢x2+a1⁢x+a0 and r1+r2=-a1a2 and r1⁢r2=a0a2. Notice that both of these formulas can be determined from the quadratic formula.

More intrestingly, if P⁢(x)=a3⁢x3+a2⁢x2+a1⁢x+a0, then r1+r2+r3=-a2a3, r1⁢r2+r2⁢r3+r3⁢r1=a1a3, and r1⁢r2⁢r3=-a0a3.
