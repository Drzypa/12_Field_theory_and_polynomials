y2=x3-2


We want to solve the equation y2=x3-2 over the integers.

By writing y2+2=x3 we can factor on ℤ⁢[-2] as

(y-i⁢2)⁢(y+i⁢2)=x3.

Using congruences modulo 8, one can show that both x,y must be odd, and it can also be shown that (y-i⁢2) and (y+i⁢2) are relatively prime (if it were not the case, any divisor would have even norm, which is not possible).

Therefore, by unique factorization, and using that the only units (http://planetmath.org/UnitsOfQuadraticFields) on ℤ⁢[-2] are 1,-1, we have that each factor must be a cube.

So let us write

(y+i⁢2)=(a+b⁢i⁢2)3=(a3-6⁢a⁢b2)+i⁢(3⁢a2⁢b-2⁢b3)⁢2

Then y=a3-6⁢a⁢b2 and 1=3⁢a2⁢b-2⁢b3=b⁢(3⁢a2-2⁢b2). These two equations imply b=±1 and thus a=±1, from where the only possible solutions are x=3,y=±5.
