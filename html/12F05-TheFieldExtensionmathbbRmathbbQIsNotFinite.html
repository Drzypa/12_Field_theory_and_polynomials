the field extension ℝ/ℚ is not finite


Theorem.

Let L/K be a finite field extension. Then L/K is an algebraic extension.

Corollary.

The extension of fields R/Q is not finite.

Proof of the Corollary.

If the extension was finite, it would be an algebraic extension. However, the extension ℝ/ℚ is clearly not algebraic. For example, e∈ℝ is transcendental over ℚ (see e is transcendental). ∎
