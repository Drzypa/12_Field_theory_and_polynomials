equivalent conditions for normality of a field extension


Theorem.

If K/F is an algebraic extension of fields, then the following are equivalent:

  1. 1.

    K is normal over F;

  2. 2.

    K is the splitting field over F of a set of polynomials in F⁢[X];

  3. 3.

    if F¯ is an algebraic closure F containing K and σ:K→F¯ is an F-monomorphism, then σ⁢(K)=K.

Proof.

(1)⇒(2) Let X be an F-basis for K, and for each x∈X, let fx be the irreducible polynomial of x over F. By hypothesis, each fx splits over K, and because we evidently have K=F⁢(X), it follows that K is a splitting field of {fx:x∈X} over F.
(2)⇒(3) Assume that K is a splitting field over F of S⊆F⁢[X]. Given f∈S, we may write f⁢(X)=u⁢∏i=1n(X-ui) for some u,u1,…,un∈K; because σ fixes F pointwise, we have σ⁢(ui)∈{u1,…,un} for 1≤i≤n, and since σ is injective, it must simply permute the roots of f. Thus u1,…,un∈σ⁢(K). As K is generated over F by the roots of the polynomials in S, we obtain K=σ⁢(K).
(3)⇒(1) Let K¯ be an algebraic closure of K, noting that, since K is algebraic over F, that same is true of K¯, and consequently K¯ is an algebraic closure of F containing K. Now suppose f∈F⁢[X] is irreducible and that u∈K is a root of f, and let v be any root of F in K¯. There exists an F-isomorphism τ:F⁢(u)→F⁢(v) such that τ⁢(u)=v. Because K¯ is a splitting field over both F⁢(u) and F⁢(v) of the set of irreducible polynomials in F⁢[X], τ extends to an F-isomorphism σ:K¯→K¯. It follows that σ|K:K→K¯ is an F-monomorphism, so that, by hypothesis, σ⁢(K)=K, hence that v=σ⁢(u)∈K. Thus f splits over K, and therefore K/F is normal. ∎
