simple field extension


Let K⁢(α) be obtained from the field K via the of the element α, which is called the primitive element of the field extension K⁢(α)/K.  We shall settle the of the field K⁢(α).

We consider the substitution homomorphism  φ:K⁢[X]→K⁢[α], where

∑aν⁢Xν↦∑aν⁢αν.

According to the ring homomorphism theorem, the image ring K⁢[α] is isomorphic with the residue class ring K⁢[X]/𝔭, where 𝔭 is the ideal of polynomials having α as their zero.  Because K⁢[α] is, as subring of the field K⁢(α), an integral domain, then also K⁢[X]/𝔭 has no zero divisors, and hence 𝔭 is a prime ideal.  It must be principal, for K⁢[X] is a principal ideal ring.

There are two possibilities:

  1. 1.

    𝔭=(p⁢(X)), where p⁢(X) is an irreducible polynomial with  p⁢(α)=0.  Because every non-zero prime ideal of K⁢[X] is maximal, the isomorphic image K⁢[X]/(p⁢(X)) of K⁢[α] is a field, and it must give the of  K⁢(α)=K⁢[α].  We say that α is algebraic with respect to K (or over K).  In this case, we have a finite field extension  K⁢(α)/K.

  2. 2.

    𝔭=(0).  This means that the homomorphism φ is an isomorphism between K⁢[X] and K⁢[α], i.e. all expressions ∑aν⁢αν behave as the polynomials ∑aν⁢Xν.  Now, K⁢[α] is no field because K⁢[X] is not such, but the isomorphy of the rings implies the isomorphy of the corresponding fields of fractions.  Thus the simple extension field K⁢(α) is isomorphic with the field K⁢(X) of rational functions in one indeterminate X.  We say that α is transcendental (http://planetmath.org/Algebraic) with respect to K (or over K).  This time we have a simple infinite field extension  K⁢(α)/K.
