variant of Cardano’s derivation


By a linear change of variable, a cubic polynomial over ℂ can be given the form x3+3⁢b⁢x+c. To find the zeros of this cubic in the form of surds in b and c, make the substitution x=y1/3+z1/3, thus replacing one unknown with two, and then write down identities which are suggested by the resulting equation in two unknowns. Specifically, we get

y+3⁢(y1/3+z1/3)⁢y1/3⁢z1/3+z+3⁢b⁢(y1/3+z1/3)+c=0. (1)

This will be true if

y+z+c=0 (2)
3⁢y1/3⁢z1/3+3⁢b=0, (3)

which in turn requires

y⁢z=-b3. (4)

The pair of equations (2) and (4) is a quadratic system in y and z, readily solved. But notice that (3) puts a restriction on a certain choice of cube roots.
